### Important Note on Potential Form (られる)

For many verbs, the actual potential form changes the verb itself:

| Dictionary Form | Potential Form | English   |
| --------------- | -------------- | --------- |
| はなす             | はなせる           | can speak |
| よむ              | よめる            | can read  |
| かく              | かける            | can write |
| およぐ             | およげる           | can swim  |

So in natural Japanese:

* にほんごがはなせる。

* I can speak Japanese.

* およげる。

* I can swim.

| Grammar | Meaning                       |

#### Examples

**てあげる**

* わたしがともだちをてつだってあげた。
* I helped my friend.

**てくれる**

* I received help from my friend.

### Important Difference

**てある**

* Result of an intentional action remains.
* ドアがしめてある。
* The door has been closed (and remains closed).

**ている**

* State or action in progress.
* ドアがしまっている。
* The door is closed.

| Grammar | Meaning                        | Focus            |
