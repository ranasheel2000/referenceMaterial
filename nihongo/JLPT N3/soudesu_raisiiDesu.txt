600を下回る見込みのようです。
- 600を → below 600
- 下回る → to fall below
- 見込み → expectation / forecast
- のようです → it seems

It seems that it is expected to fall below 600.

Explanation:

見込み（みこみ / mikomi）
English: Expectation, forecast, prospect, prediction
It is commonly used in news reports and formal Japanese.

Examples:
- 増える見込みです。
It is expected to increase.
- 完成する見込みです。
It is expected to be completed.

～見込みです
Meaning: It is expected that...
Pattern:
- Verb (dictionary form) + 見込みです
Examples:
 来年完成する見込みです。
It is expected to be completed next year.
- 利用者は増える見込みです。
The number of users is expected to increase.

～ようです
English: It seems..., Apparently...
This expresses the speaker's judgment based on evidence or available information.
Pattern:
- Verb / Adjective / Noun + の + ようです (for nouns)
Example:
- 雨が降るようです。
It seems it's going to rain.
- 事故だったようです。
It seems it was an accident.




yodesu soudesu raishi
These three expressions are often confused because they can all be translated as "seems" or "looks like," but they are used differently.

| Grammar    | Meaning                       | Based on                                        | Example    | English                           |
| ---------- | ----------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- | ---------- | --------------------------------- |
| **～ようです**  | It seems...                   | Evidence, observation, or reasoning             | 雨が降るようです。  | It seems it's going to rain.      |
| **～そうです**  | It looks like... (appearance) | What you can see right now                      | 雨が降りそうです。  | It looks like it's about to rain. |
| **～そうです**  | I heard that... (hearsay)     | Information from someone else                   | 雨が降るそうです。  | I heard it's going to rain.       |
| **～らしいです** | Apparently... / It seems...   | Reports, reputation, or typical characteristics | 雨が降るらしいです。 | Apparently, it's going to rain.   |

## 1. ～ようです (It seems...)

Used when **you make your own judgment based on evidence**.

**Example**

* 雲が黒いです。雨が降るようです。
* **English:** The clouds are dark. It seems it's going to rain.

Another example:

* 彼は忙しいようです。
* **English:** He seems busy.

---

## 2. ～そうです (Looks like...)

Used when something **looks like it is about to happen**.

**Example**

* 雨が降りそうです。
* **English:** It looks like it's about to rain.

Another example:

* このケーキはおいしそうです。
* **English:** This cake looks delicious.

Notice:

* This is based on **appearance**, not information from others.

---

## 3. ～そうです (I heard...)

This is a different grammar pattern with the same pronunciation.

Used when **someone told you**.

**Example**

* 天気予報では、雨が降るそうです。
* **English:** According to the weather forecast, I heard it's going to rain.

Another example:

* 彼は来月結婚するそうです。
* **English:** I heard he's getting married next month.

---

## 4. ～らしいです (Apparently...)

Used when **you've heard information from various sources** or when something has the **typical characteristics** of something.

**Example (report)**

* 彼は来ないらしいです。
* **English:** Apparently, he isn't coming.

**Example (typical characteristic)**

* 春らしい天気です。
* **English:** It's spring-like weather.

---

## Easy way to remember

| Grammar                | Think of it as...                              |
| ---------------------- | ---------------------------------------------- |
| **～そうです** (appearance) | **Looks like...** 👀                           |
| **～そうです** (hearsay)    | **I heard...** 👂                              |
| **～ようです**              | **I think... because of evidence.** 🧠         |
| **～らしいです**             | **Apparently... / From what I've heard...** 📰 |

### Example with one situation (rain)

* **雨が降りそうです。**

  * **English:** It looks like it's about to rain. (You see dark clouds.)

* **雨が降るようです。**

  * **English:** It seems it's going to rain. (You conclude this from the weather.)

* **雨が降るそうです。**

  * **English:** I heard it's going to rain. (Someone or the forecast said so.)

* **雨が降るらしいです。**

  * **English:** Apparently, it's going to rain. (That's what people are saying.)

This distinction is part of **JLPT N3** and is one of the most frequently tested grammar comparisons.
