# JLPT N3 - Day 2: express decisions, logical conclusions, explanations, denials, constraints, and exceptions.

## 1. ことになる (koto ni naru)

### Meaning
Something that has been decided (～ことに決まった)

### Key Points
- Indicates that something is decided **regardless of one's own will**
- Can also be used when you've made your own decision, but you want to state it indirectly or euphemistically (婉曲的えんきょくてきに)
- Most commonly used in the **past tense**: ことになった
- Emphasizes that a decision has been finalized, often by external factors

### Examples
- 来月らいげつ、引ひっ越こしすることになりました。
  - *I'm going to move next month.* (formal/settled announcement)
- 来週らいしゅうの会議かいぎは、オンラインで行おこなうことになった。
  - *Next week's meeting will be held online.* (decided by the organization)
- 今年ことしは、イベントをしないことになりました。
  - *This year, there won't be an event.* (external decision)

## 2. なぜなら～からだ (nazenara... karada)

### Meaning
It's because ~ / The reason is ~. Used to explain the cause or reason for something previously mentioned.

### Key Points
- Used in **written language** and **formal spoken situations**
- In **daily conversation**, the informal alternative is: **どうしてかというと～からだ**
- Provides a formal, clear explanation of cause or reason

### Examples
- 私わたくしは日本語にほんごを勉強べんきょうしています。なぜなら、仕事しごとで日本語にほんごを使つかうからです。
  - *I am studying Japanese. The reason why is because I use it at work.*
- 駅前えきまえの新あたしいレストランは人気にんきがある。なぜなら、料理りょうりもサービスも非常ひじょうにいいからだ。
  - *The new restaurant in front of the station is very popular. The reason why is because the food and service are very good.*
- 毎日まいにち勉強べんきょうしたほうがいい。なぜなら、学生がくせいだからだ。
  - *You should study every day, because you are a student.*

### Casual Alternative
- 私わたしは漫画家まんがかになりたい。どうしてかというと、漫画まんがが好すきだからだ。
  - *I want to be a manga artist. The reason why is because I love manga.* (casual)

## 3. わけだ (wake da)

### Meaning
That's why ~ / No wonder ~ / Which means ~. A variety of reasons naturally lead to a conclusion.

### Key Points
- Shows that you **understand the reason** for something and are satisfied
- In **spoken language**, noun forms can shift: Noun + の → Noun + な (わけだ → なわけだ)
- Often used with: **だから** (it's because), **なるほど** (indeed), **どうりで** (no wonder)
- Expresses logical acceptance and natural understanding

### Examples
- 20ページの宿題しゅくだいだから、1日にちに2ページずつすれば、10日かで終おわるわけだ。
  - *It's a 20 pages homework assignment, so if I do two pages a day, it should be done in 10 days.*
- 10日ひ後ごに届とどくということだから、届とどくのは来週らいしゅうの月曜日げつようびのわけだ。
  - *It will be delivered in 10 days, which means it should arrive next Monday.*
- A：ケビンさんのお父とうさんは日本人にほんじんなんだって。
  - *He/She said Kevin's father is Japanese.*
- B：どうりでケビンさんの日本語にほんごの発音はつおんがきれいなわけだ。
  - *No wonder Kevin's Japanese pronunciation is so beautiful.*
- 暑あついわけだ。気温きおんが38度どもある。
  - *No wonder it's so hot. It's 38 degrees (Celsius).*

## 4. わけではない (wake de wa nai)

### Meaning
Not all / It's not necessarily true. You can't say that all of them are ~.

### Key Points
- Indicates **partial negation** — something might be partly true, but not entirely or necessarily
- Often paired with: **～からといって** (just because)
- **Special form**: ～ないわけではない = partial affirmation ("It's not that I don't..., but...")
- In **spoken language**, noun forms can shift: Noun + の → Noun + な

### Examples
- 彼かれが言いったことのすべてが嘘うそだったわけではない。
  - *Not everything he said was a lie.*
- お金かね持もちの人ひとがみんな幸しあわせなわけではない。
  - *Not all rich people are happy.*
- インターネットのニュースはいつも正ただしいわけではない。
  - *Internet news isn't always right.*
- 日本人にほんじんがみんな敬語けいごが上手じょうずに使つかえるわけではない。
  - *Not all Japanese can use keigo well.*

### Partial Affirmation (～ないわけではない)
- テレビを見みないわけではないが、インターネットを見みているほうが多おおい。
  - *It's not that I don't watch TV, but I watch the Internet more often.*
- お酒さけが飲のめないわけではありませんが、あまり好すきではありません。
  - *It's not that I can't drink alcohol, but I don't really like it.*

## 5. わけがない (wake ga nai)

### Meaning
There is no way that ~. It's absolutely impossible.

### Key Points
- Used for **strong denial** when something is absolutely unthinkable
- Can be replaced by: **～はずがない** (shouldn't be / cannot be)
- In **spoken language**, it's often shortened to: **わけない**
- In **spoken language**, noun forms can shift: Noun + の → Noun + な
- **Double negative rule**: Negative form + わけがない = strong confidence that something is true

### Examples
- 事件じけんのあった日ひ、私わたしと一緒いっしょにいたのだから、彼女かのじょが犯人はんにんのわけがない。
  - *She was with me on the day of the incident, so there was no way she was the culprit.*
- 毎日まいにちゲームをしている彼女かのじょが、ゲームを嫌きらいなわけがない。
  - *She plays games every day; how could she not like games?* (She definitely likes games)
- 高級こうきゅうブランドの時計とけいだから、そんなに安やすいわけがない。
  - *It's a luxury brand watch, so it can't be cheap.*
- こんな難むずかしい問題もんだい、こどもに分わかるわけがない。
  - *How can a child understand such a difficult problem?*
- 全然ぜんぜん勉強べんきょうしてないんだから、合格ごうかくするわけないよ。
  - *You're not studying at all, so there's no way you'll pass.* (casual)

## 6. わけにはいかない (wake ni wa ikanai)

### Meaning
I want to, but I can't due to social or psychological reasons.

### Key Points
- Used when you **have a desire to do something (or not do it), but cannot due to social/psychological constraints**
- **Cannot be used** for personal circumstances or physical inability (e.g., "can't eat because teeth hurt" ✖️)
- **Negative form rule**: Verb (negative) + わけにはいかない = "I don't want to do it, but I must because it's required/natural"
- Emphasizes obligation due to external pressure, not personal ability

### Examples
- 明日あしたは大事だいじな面接めんせつがあるから休やすむわけにはいかない。
  - *I have an important interview tomorrow, so I can't miss it.* (social obligation)
- 社長しゃちょうの誘さそいだから、断ことわるわけにはいかない。
  - *Since it was an invitation from the president, I couldn't refuse it.* (social pressure)
- 今日きょうは大事だいじな会議かいぎがあるから絶対ぜったいに遅おくれるわけにはいかない。
  - *I have an important meeting today, so I definitely can't be late.* (obligation)
- 行いくと約束やくそくしたのだから、行いかないわけにはいかない。
  - *I promised to go, so I can't not go.* (commitment)

## 7. ずにはいられない (zu ni wa irarenai)

### Meaning
Couldn't help but ~. An uncontrollable action or reaction you can't avoid.

### Key Points
- Used for **spontaneous reactions** where you cannot stand a situation and cannot help but do something
- Can be replaced by: **～ないではいられない**
- **Special verb rule**: For する (to do), the form becomes: **せずにはいられない**
- Reflects **internal impulse or physical sensation**, not external constraint

### Examples
- 読よんでいたマンガが面白おもしろくて、笑わらわずにはいられない。
  - *The manga I was reading was so funny, I couldn't help but laugh.* (involuntary)
- 蚊かに刺さされたところがかゆくて、かかずにはいられない。
  - *The mosquito bites are so itchy that I can't help but scratch them.* (physical reflex)
- 私わたしは分わからないことがあると、すぐに調しらべずにはいられない。
  - *Whenever I don't understand something, I can't help but look it up right away.* (impulse)
- 台風たいふうの被害者ひがいしゃのことを思おもうと、早はやく復興ふっこうすることを願ねがわずにはいられません。
  - *When I think of the victims of the typhoon, I can't help but hope that they will recover soon.* (emotional)

## 8. とは限らない (to wa kagiranai)

### Meaning
Not necessarily ~. It is not always true; exceptions exist.

### Key Points
- Used to say that **something is not always true** and that exceptions exist
- Clarifies that **general assumptions don't always apply**
- Often paired with adverbs: **いつも** (always), **全部** (all), **誰でも** (anyone), **必ずしも** (not necessarily)
- Often used with: **～からといって** (just because)

### Examples
- 新聞しんぶんに書かいてあることが必かならずしも事実じじつとは限かぎらない。
  - *What is written in the newspaper is not always true.*
- お金かね持もちがいつも幸しあわせとは限かぎらない。
  - *Rich people are not always happy.*
- 値段ねだんの高たかいものが全部ぜんぶいいものとは限かぎらない。
  - *Not everything that is expensive is good.*
- 日本人にほんじんだからといって、誰だれでもN1の漢字かんじが全すべて書かけるとは限かぎらない。
  - *Just because you are Japanese does not mean that everyone can write all N1 kanji.*

## Grammar Point Comparisons

### わけだ vs. わけがない

| Feature | わけだ | わけがない |
|---------|--------|-----------|
| **Meaning** | That's why / No wonder | There is no way / Impossible |
| **Tone** | Logical acceptance | Strong denial |
| **Nuance** | "It makes sense that..." | "It is absolutely unthinkable that..." |
| **Example** | "No wonder it's hot; it's 38°C" | "She was with me; there's no way she's the culprit" |

---

### わけだ vs. わけではない

| Feature | わけだ | わけではない |
|---------|--------|-----------|
| **Core Function** | Confirms logical result | Denies total/absolute truth |
| **Meaning** | "That explains it" / "Naturally" | "It's not necessarily the case" |
| **Tone** | Understanding and satisfaction | Clarifying an exception |
| **Example** | "No wonder it's not opening; wrong key" | "Not all rich people are happy" |

---

### ずにはいられない vs. わけにはいかない

| Feature | ずにはいられない | わけにはいかない |
|---------|------------------|------------------|
| **Source of Action** | Internal impulse / Physical sensation | Social responsibility / Psychological pressure |
| **Nuance** | "I can't stop myself" | "I'm not allowed to" |
| **Physical Ability** | Can describe reactions (e.g., scratching) | Cannot describe physical inability (e.g., pain) |
| **Example** | "So funny I couldn't help laughing" | "Important meeting; I can't be late" |
