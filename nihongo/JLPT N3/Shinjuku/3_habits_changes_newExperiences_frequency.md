# JLPT N3 - Day 3: Grammar, Vocabulary & Kanji

## Overview
Day 3 covers nine essential grammar structures that express past habits, changes, new experiences, and frequency, along with key vocabulary and kanji for everyday situations.

---

## Grammar

## 1. ～たものだ (ta mono da)

### Meaning
An expression that reminds you of something you used to do well in the past.

### Key Points
- Expresses **past habits or recurring actions** you recall fondly
- Often used with adverbs of frequency: **よく** (often), **しばしば** (oftentimes), **しょっちゅう** (frequently)
- Most commonly used in past tense to reflect on what you used to do

### Examples
- 子供こどもの頃ころは、よく川かわで泳およいだものだ。
  - *When I was a kid, I used to swim in the river often.*
- 学生がくせいの頃ころは、朝あさまで友達ともだちと遊あそんだものだ。
  - *When I was in school, I used to play with my friends until morning.*
- 夏休なつやすみに家族かぞくで旅行りょこうしたものだが、大学生だいがくせいになってからは全然ぜんぜんしていない。
  - *I used to travel with my family during summer vacation, but I haven't done that at all since I became a college student.*

---

## 2. 一方だ (ippo da)

### Meaning
(The state/situation is) progressing rapidly in the direction of ~.

### Key Points
- Connects with **verbs that express change** (変化へんか動詞どうし)
- Can be used for both **positive and negative changes**, but more often used for **negative changes**
- Emphasizes a continuing trend or progression

### Examples
- 勉強べんきょうしていないので、成績せいせきは下さがる一方いっぽうです。
  - *I'm not studying, so my grades are going down (and continuing to).*
- 悪天候あくてんこうのせいで、野菜やさいの値段ねだんは上あがる一方いっぽうです。
  - *Due to bad weather, the price of vegetables is only going up.*
- 地球ちきゅう温暖化おんだんかは進すすむ一方いっぽうだ。
  - *Global warming is only going to get worse.*

---

## 3. ～ば～ほど (ba... hodo)

### Meaning
If you do A, you get B. If you do more A, you get B more. When the degree of A changes, B changes with it.

### Key Points
- The consequent (result) must be a **change verb**: 増ふえる (increase), 減へる (decrease), 変かわる (change), ～になる (become), ～くる (come)
- The first "～ば" part can be **omitted**: "～ほど" alone is acceptable
  - Example: 漢字かんじは書かくほど、覚おぼえやすくなる。(The more kanji you write, the easier it is to remember.)

### Examples
- 北きたへ行いけば行いくほど、寒さむくなります。
  - *The farther north you go, the colder it gets.*
- メールの返信へんしんは早はやければ早はやいほどいい。
  - *The sooner you reply to an email, the better.*
- お刺身さしみは新鮮しんせんならば新鮮しんせんなほどおいしいです。
  - *The fresher the sashimi, the more delicious it is.*

---

## 4. ～てはじめて (te hajimete)

### Meaning
～And then, for the first time in my life... An expression of what you have learned or noticed after experiencing something for the first time.

### Key Points
- Expresses **initial experiences** and realizations you didn't have before
- Common phrases after ～てはじめて:
  - ～てはじめて + **気がつく・気づく** (realize)
  - ～てはじめて + **わかる** (understand)
  - ～てはじめて + **知る** (know)
- **No volitional or command expressions** come after this structure

### Examples
- 日本にほんへ来きてはじめて、満開まんかいの桜さくらを見みました。
  - *It was only after I came to Japan that I saw cherry blossoms in full bloom.*
- 病気びょうきになってはじめて、食事しょくじの大切たいせつさに気きづきました。
  - *Only after I became ill did I realize the importance of meals.*
- アルバイトをしてはじめて、働はたらくことの大変たいへんさがわかりました。
  - *I realized how hard it is to work when I started working part-time.*

---

## 5. ～てばかりいる (te bakari iru)

### Meaning
Always doing B / Lots of the same things / Doing the same things over and over.

### Key Points
- Often used in a **negative sense** (but can be neutral)
- Indicates repetition or excess of the same action
- Expresses a critical or concerned tone

### Examples
- 弟おとうとは勉強べんきょうしないで、遊あそんでばかりいる。
  - *My brother doesn't study; he just plays around.*
- 休やすみの日ひは寝ねてばかりいる。
  - *On my days off, I just sleep.*
- 一人ひとり暮ぐらしをしている息子むすこはインスタント食品しょくひんばかり食たべているので、心配しんぱいだ。
  - *I'm worried about my son who lives alone and eats only instant food.*
- 大学生だいがくせいの姉あねは、大学だいがくに通かよわず、バイトばかりしている。
  - *My older sister, who is a college student, does not attend classes and works only part-time.*

---

## 6. ～ぶりに～ (buri ni)

### Meaning
After the period of A has passed, do B again. Used to say "I did something again that I didn't do for a long time."

### Key Points
- Used when the **speaker feels that a long time has passed**
- Related phrase: **久ひさしぶり** (it's been a long time) — used as a greeting to someone you haven't seen in ages
- Emphasizes the duration since the last time

### Examples
- 今日きょう、５年ねんぶりに高校こうこうのクラスメイトに会あいました。
  - *Today, I met my high school classmates for the first time in five years.*
- ２週間しゅうかんぶりの雨あめが、降ふりました。
  - *It rained for the first time in 2 weeks.*
- A：久ひさしぶり〜！
  - *It's been a while!*
- B：久ひさしぶりだね！大学だいがくの卒業式そつぎょうしきぶりだね。
  - *Long time no see! It's been since our college graduation ceremony.*

---

## 7. ～ごとに～ (goto ni)

### Meaning
When in A, always in B / In A units.

### Key Points
- Often used for things that have **regularity or repetition**
- Example: 100円えん買かい物ものするごとに、1ポイントもらえる。(For every 100 yen you spend, you get 1 point.)
- Indicates a fixed interval or condition

### Examples
- このアラームは5分ふんごとに鳴なる。
  - *This alarm goes off every five minutes.*
- A、B、C、D 各かくグループごとに集あつまってください。
  - *Please gather in groups A, B, C, and D.*
- このライトはボタンを押おすごとに、色いろが変かわります。
  - *This light changes color each time you press the button.*

---

## 8. ～たびに～ (tabi ni)

### Meaning
When in A, always in B / When (A) happens, then it's always the same thing (B).

### Key Points
- **Cannot be used** for natural/habitual things (unlike ～ごとに)
  - ✖️ 毎朝まいあさ起おきるたびに、コーヒーを飲のむ。 (Don't use for daily habits)
- **No negative or adjectival sentences** in the consequent
  - ✖️ 父ちちは外国がいこくへ行いくたびに、お土産みやげを買かわない。 (Wrong)
- Used for **specific, notable occurrences**

### Examples
- コンビニで新商品しんしょうひんを見みるたびに、つい買かってしまう。
  - *Whenever I see a new product at a convenience store, I end up buying it.*
- この曲きょくを聞きくたびに、高校時代こうこうじだいを思おもい出だす。
  - *Whenever I hear this song, it reminds me of my high school days.*
- 引越ひっこしのたびに、物ものが増ふえていく。
  - *Every time I move, my stuff increases.*

---

## 9. ～ところが～ (tokoro ga)

### Meaning
A. However, B. / A but B. Indicates that B is contrary to expectations from A.

### Key Points
- **No volitional expressions** (～よう, ～たい) in the consequent
- Expresses **surprise or contradiction**
- A **conjunction** connecting two independent clauses

### Examples
- 声こえがしたので、部屋へやを開あけた。ところが、部屋へやには誰だれもいなかった。
  - *I heard a voice, so I opened the room. However, there was no one in the room.*

## Additional Vocabulary Items

### なるべく (narubeku)
**Meaning:** As much as possible / as best as one can

- 健康けんこうのために、車くるまを使つかわず、なるべく歩あるくようにしている。
  - *For my health, I try to walk as much as possible instead of using the car.*

---

## Vocabulary

### すでに (already)
**Meaning:** Before / Already

- 空港くうこうに着ついた時とき、飛行機ひこうきはすでに出でた後あとだった。
  - *When I arrived at the airport, the plane had already left.*

---

### 気になる (ki ni naru)
**Meaning:** Feel uneasy / Be worried / Be interested

Meanings:
1. Things that make you worry
2. Things I can't get out of my head
3. Be interested in

- 明日あしたのテストが気きになって、眠ねむれない。
  - *I can't sleep because I'm worried about tomorrow's test.*
- 最近さいきん、シティ・ポップが気きになっている。
  - *City pop has been on my mind lately.*

---

### さらに (sarani)
**Meaning:** Furthermore / On top of that / More

- この部屋へやは安やすい。さらに、駅えきからも近ちかくて便利べんりだ。
  - *This room is cheap. In addition, it is conveniently located near the station.*
- 年としを取とって彼女かのじょはさらに美うつくしくなった。
  - *As she got older, she became even more beautiful.*

---

### 身につける (mi ni tsukeru)
**Meaning:** Carry / Learn / Acquire knowledge

Meanings:
1. To wear, to put on, or to put on the body
2. Learn knowledge and skills, and make them your own

- 彼女かのじょは不思議ふしぎな形かたちのイヤリングを身みにつけていた。
  - *She was wearing a pair of strangely shaped earrings.*
- 最近さいきんの技術ぎじゅつを身みにつけたいと思おもう。
  - *I want to learn the latest technology.*

---

### ずっと (zutto)
**Meaning:** All the way / For a long time / Much more

- 駅前えきまえで彼女かのじょをずっと待まっていたが、来こなかった。
  - *I kept waiting for her in front of the station, but she never came.*
- 弟おとうとは、私わたしよりも 背せがずっと高たかい。
  - *My brother is much taller than I am.*

---

### 結構 (kekkou)
**Meaning:** Fine / Quite / Enough

Meanings:
1. Sufficient / Quite
2. That's good
3. I don't need it (polite refusal)

- あの店みせの料理りょうりは、結構けっこうおいしかった。
  - *The food at that restaurant was pretty good.*
- A：これで、よろしいでしょうか。
  - *Now, are you sure?*
- B：はい、結構けっこうです。（＝良よい）
  - *Yes, thank you. (= good)*
- A：コーヒーは、いかがでしょうか。
  - *Would you like some coffee?*
- B：いいえ、結構けっこうです。
  - *No, thank you. (= I don't want it.)*

---

### この頃 (kono koro)
**Meaning:** These days / Recently

- この頃ごろは、忙いそがしくて趣味しゅみの登山とざんに行いくことができない。
  - *I am too busy these days to go mountain climbing as a hobby.*

---

### 絶対に (zettai ni)
**Meaning:** Absolutely / Never (depending on context)

Meanings:
1. No matter what happens, I will always do it
2. Never (with negative)

- 明日あしたの試験しけんは、絶対ぜったいに遅刻ちこくしないでください。
  - *Please do not be late for tomorrow's exam under any circumstances.*
- 引ひっ越こししても、皆みんなのことは絶対ぜったいに忘わすれない。
  - *Even if I move, I will never forget everyone.*
