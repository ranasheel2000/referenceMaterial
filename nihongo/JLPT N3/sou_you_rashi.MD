# ～そうです vs ～ようです vs ～らしいです

## Quick Comparison

| Grammar                | Core Meaning                    | Information Source                          | Speaker's Confidence | Typical Use                                         |
| ---------------------- | ------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------- | -------------------- | --------------------------------------------------- |
| **～そうです** (Appearance) | **Looks like...**               | What you see **right now**                  | Low–Medium           | Immediate visual impression                         |
| **～ようです**              | **It seems... / It appears...** | Your own observation + reasoning + evidence | Medium               | Personal inference                                  |
| **～そうです** (Hearsay)    | **I heard that...**             | Someone else told you                       | Not your opinion     | Reporting information                               |
| **～らしいです**             | **Apparently...**               | Information from others, reports, rumors    | Medium               | Passing on information you believe is probably true |

---

# 1. ～そうです (Appearance)

### Meaning

**It looks like...**

### Use when:

You judge something from **what you can directly see right now.**

Think:

> 👀 **"My eyes tell me this."**

### Examples

**雨が降りそうです。**

> It looks like it's going to rain.

(You see dark clouds.)

---

**このケーキはおいしそうです。**

> This cake looks delicious.

(You haven't tasted it.)

---

## Key Point

This grammar is about **appearance**, **not reasoning**.

---

# 2. ～ようです

### Meaning

**It seems... / It appears...**

### Use when:

You **combine evidence with your own reasoning**.

Think:

> 🧠 **"The evidence makes me think..."**

You don't have to literally see it.

Evidence may include:

* what you see
* what you hear
* circumstances
* logical reasoning

---

### Examples

**電気が消えています。誰もいないようです。**

> The lights are off. It seems no one is home.

You didn't see inside.

You inferred it.

---

**足跡があります。誰か来たようです。**

> There are footprints. It seems someone came.

You conclude this from evidence.

---

### Key Point

Unlike **そうです (appearance)**,

you are **reasoning**, not merely describing what your eyes see. ([Japanese Language Stack Exchange][1])

---

# 3. ～そうです (Hearsay)

### Meaning

**I heard...**

### Use when:

You are **reporting** what someone else said.

Think:

> 👂 **"Someone told me."**

---

### Examples

**天気予報では、雨が降るそうです。**

> According to the weather forecast, I heard it will rain.

---

**彼は来月結婚するそうです。**

> I heard he's getting married next month.

---

### Key Point

No personal judgment.

You're simply passing along information.

---

# 4. ～らしいです

### Meaning

**Apparently...**

### Use when:

Information comes from

* reports
* rumors
* several people
* something you read or heard

Think:

> 📰 **"That's what people are saying."**

---

### Examples

**彼は会社を辞めたらしいです。**

> Apparently he quit his job.

---

**来週から寒くなるらしいです。**

> Apparently it'll get colder next week.

---

### Another important use

**らしい** also means

> "typical of"

Example:

**子どもらしい。**

> Childlike.

**春らしい天気です。**

> It's spring-like weather.

---

# The Rain Example

Suppose rain is coming.

### 👀 You look outside.

**雨が降りそうです。**

"It looks like it's about to rain."

You are judging only from the sky.

---

### 🧠 You check several signs.

Dark clouds.

Wind.

Humidity.

Radar.

**雨が降るようです。**

"It seems it's going to rain."

Your conclusion is based on evidence.

---

### 👂 Someone tells you.

Weather forecast:

**雨が降るそうです。**

"I heard it's going to rain."

---

### 📰 Everyone is saying it.

Friends.

News.

Internet.

**雨が降るらしいです。**

"Apparently it's going to rain."

---

# Memory Trick

| Grammar                   | Think                               |
| ------------------------- | ----------------------------------- |
| 👀 **～そうです** (Appearance) | **I SEE it.**                       |
| 🧠 **～ようです**              | **I THINK so because of evidence.** |
| 👂 **～そうです** (Hearsay)    | **I HEARD it.**                     |
| 📰 **～らしいです**             | **People say so. Apparently.**      |

# One Subtle Difference (N3)

Many learners ask:

> **If both ～そうです (hearsay) and ～らしいです come from other people, what's the difference?**

A useful guideline is:

* **～そうです** = you're **quoting** the source. You don't express your own judgment.
* **～らしいです** = you're passing along information that you've heard or gathered, and it often sounds like you consider it **probably true**, though you're still not claiming certainty. ([Japanese Language Stack Exchange][1])

For example:

* **天気予報では、雨が降るそうです。**

  * *The weather forecast says it will rain.*

* **明日は雨らしいです。**

  * *Apparently it's going to rain tomorrow.*

The first emphasizes **the source**; the second emphasizes **the information**.

This distinction is the one that most JLPT N3 textbooks and native explanations rely on.
