## 1. **～なくちゃ / ～なきゃ**

(Shortened form of ～なければならない / "must do")

### **Meaning**

This is a **shortened, informal** way to say **「～なければならない」** or **「～ないといけない」**, which both mean "must do" or "have to do."

* **V (negative form) + なくちゃ / なきゃ**  
  This form is often used in **casual conversations** and is more **colloquial**.

### **Examples**

* **勉強しなきゃ** (I must study)
* **試験まであと1ヶ月だ。がんばって勉強しないと。**  
  There’s only one month until the exam. I must study hard.
* **田中さんにメールの返信をしなくちゃ。**  
  I have to reply to Tanaka’s email.

### **Note:**

* The **～なくちゃ** and **～なきゃ** forms are **casual** and common in everyday speech.
* **しなきゃ / しなくちゃ** = **しなければならない**  
  (must do)

## 2. **～ちゃう / ～じゃう**

(Informal way of saying **～てしまう** / "to do something completely")

### **Meaning**

This is a casual, **colloquial** expression for **～てしまう**, which implies the action is done completely or unexpectedly, often with a sense of regret or emphasis.

* **V (て form) + ちゃう / じゃう**

### **Examples**

* **食べちゃった** (I ate it all / I ate it by mistake)
* **あ、食べちゃった。いけなかった？**  
  Oh, I ate it. Was that bad?
* **試験が終わった!今日は飲んじゃおう!**  
  The exam is over! Let's drink today!  
  (The emphasis is on the feeling of relief or excitement.)
* **車が混んじゃって、少し遅れます。**  
  The traffic is so bad, I’ll be a little late.  
  (The use of **ちゃう** suggests a sense of something happening unexpectedly or something out of your control.)

### **Note:**

* The use of **ちゃう / じゃう** adds a **casual, emotional** or **unexpected nuance** to the verb action.
* This form is common in **everyday spoken Japanese** and often used to convey that the action happened by accident or with emphasis.

## 3. **～とく / ～ときます / ～とくる**

(Informal form of **～ておく** / "to do something in advance")

### **Meaning**

This is a **shortened** and **casual** version of **～ておく**, which means to do something **in advance** or **to prepare for something**.

* **V (て form) + とく / ときます**  
  Used when someone plans to do something **in advance** for a later need.

### **Examples**

* **テストで間違ったところをノートに書いとこう。**  
  Let’s write down the mistakes from the test in the notebook.  
  (This is an action taken to prepare for the future.)
* **お母さん、これ洗濯しといて。**  
  Mom, please do the laundry.  
  (This is asking someone to **do something in advance**.)
* **トイレットペーパーがもうすぐなくなるから、買っとかないと。**  
  We’re running out of toilet paper, so I must buy some in advance.

### **Note:**

* **～とく** and **～ときます** are used informally in conversations to describe **preparatory actions** that need to be done before something happens.
* **～ておく** means **to do something in preparation**, and **～とく** is just a **casual form**.

---

## 4. **～ちゃう / ～じゃう** (Casual form of てしまう)

### **Meaning**

As mentioned earlier, this form is used to describe something that is **completed or done completely**, and often carries a nuance of something happening unexpectedly or regrettably.

* **V (て form) + ちゃう / じゃう**

### **Examples**

* **試験が終わった! 今日は飲んじゃおう!**  
  The exam is over! Let's drink today!  
  (It expresses excitement about doing something because the exam is finished.)
* **車が混んじゃって、少し遅れます。**  
  The traffic is so bad, I’ll be a little late.

---

### **Quick Summary of Key Patterns**

1. **～なくちゃ / ～なきゃ**
   * Meaning: **must do** / **have to do**
   * Example: **勉強しなきゃ** (I must study)

2. **～ちゃう / ～じゃう**
   * Meaning: **to do something completely / by accident / with regret**
   * Example: **食べちゃった** (I ate it all by accident)

3. **～とく / ～ときます**
   * Meaning: **to do something in advance / to prepare**
   * Example: **洗濯しといて** (Please do the laundry in advance)

---

### **Comparison of Casual Forms:**

* **～なくちゃ / なきゃ**: Must do (informal).
* **～ちゃう / じゃう**: Indicates completion or unexpected actions, often with a sense of regret or emphasis.
* **～とく / ときます**: Do something in advance, often for preparation.
