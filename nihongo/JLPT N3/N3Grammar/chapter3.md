# **N3 Chapter 3**

**「みたいだ」「～みたいに／みたいな」「～らしい（Nらしい）」**

---

## 1. ～みたいだ

(looks like / seems like / resembles)

### **Meaning**

“**It looks like / It seems like / It’s like…**”

Used when:
1. **Things look or sound similar**
2. **You make a guess based on what you see/hear**
3. **You compare one thing to another** (metaphor)

It is **casual** and widely used in **spoken Japanese**.  
Softer and more conversational than **ようだ**.

---

## **Formation**

| Form                     | Example  | Notes                     |
| ------------------------ | -------- | ------------------------- |
| **Verb (plain) + みたいだ**  | 雨が降るみたいだ | “looks like it will rain” |
| **い-adj (plain) + みたいだ** | 寒いみたいだ   |                           |
| **な-adj (no だ) + みたいだ**  | 静かみたいだ   | no “だ”                    |
| **Noun (no だ) + みたいだ**   | 猫みたいだ    | no “だ”                    |

### Other forms

* **みたいに** → used before **verbs/adjectives**
* **みたいな + noun** → used before **nouns**

---

## **Examples**

### **A. Resemblance**

**あの鳥の鳴き声はネコみたいだ。**  
The bird’s cry **sounds like a cat**.

### **B. Manner (みたいに)**

**その鳥は猫みたいに鳴く。**  
The bird cries **like a cat**.

### **C. As a modifier (みたいなN)**

**ここの砂は星みたいな形をしている。**  
The sand here is shaped **like stars**.

### **D. Guess / speculation**

**このアパートは誰も住んでいないみたいだ。**  
It **seems like** nobody lives in this apartment.

**明日は雨みたいね。**  
It **looks like** it will rain tomorrow.

### **E. Comparing with people**

**リンさんみたいに日本語がうまくなりたい。**  
I want to become good at Japanese **like Lin**.

---

## 2. ～らしい (Nらしい)

(typical of / characteristic of / “like a real…”)

### **Meaning**

Expresses **typical characteristics** — something is exactly what people expect from that person/thing.  
= “**typical of ~** / **like a real ~** / **~-like**”

This pattern is **not** the same as the “I heard that ~” version of らしい.  
Here we focus on **Nらしい**, meaning *characteristic / typical*.

---

## **Formation**

### **Noun + らしい**

No other forms in this meaning.

---

## **Examples**

### **A. Seasonal**

**今日は春らしい暖かい日でした。**  
Today was a warm day **typical of spring**.

### **B. Characteristic**

**私は女性らしい服はあまり着ない。**  
I don’t wear very **feminine (woman-like)** clothes.

### **C. Common expressions**

* 子どもらしい — childlike
* 男らしい — manly
* 女らしい — womanly
* 君らしい — like *you* / true to *yourself*
* 私らしい — “my style” / true to me

**君らしい答えだね。**  
That’s a very “**you-like**” answer.

---

## 3. Summary: みたいだ vs. らしい

| Grammar       | Meaning                             | Usage                                      |
| ------------- | ----------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------ |
| **みたいだ**      | looks like / seems like / resembles | Casual, spoken, visual guess or comparison |
| **みたいに／みたいな** | like / similar to                   | For modifying verbs or nouns               |
| **Nらしい**      | typical of / characteristic of      | Describes typical traits, not guesswork    |

**Key difference:**

* **みたいだ** = *subjective impression / appearance*
* **らしい** = *typical qualities*

# 9. ～っぽい (looks like / has the characteristics of / tends to be)

### **Meaning**

The pattern **～っぽい** is used to describe something that **resembles** or **has the characteristics of** something else, often with a **slight nuance of a tendency or appearance**. It can also be used to describe something that feels or seems like a certain thing, often with a subjective or casual tone.

### **Formation**

* **Noun + っぽい**  
  (Used to describe things, people, or behaviors that appear or seem to be like something.)

### **Usage**

* **For describing characteristics**: Used when something or someone resembles a certain quality or appearance.
* **For tendencies**: Used to describe tendencies or things that are prone to have certain qualities.
* **For appearance or style**: Used to describe the overall look or style of something.

### **Examples**

1. **大人っぽい** (adult-like, mature)  
   **あの小学生は、大人っぽい。**  
   That elementary school student is **mature** (adult-like).  
   (It implies that the student behaves or looks older than their age.)

   **あの小学生は大人のような振る舞いをする。**  
   That elementary school student behaves **like an adult**.

2. **油っぽい** (oily, greasy)  
   **この料理は油っぽくていやだ。**  
   I don't like this dish because it's **too oily**.  
   (It describes food with a lot of oil, giving an impression of being greasy.)

   **この料理は好きじゃない。脂っこすぎる。**  
   I don’t like this dish. It’s **too greasy**.

3. **子どもっぽい** (childish, immature)  
   **れい子は子どもっぽい。**  
   Reiko is **childish** (acting like a child).  
   (It implies that the person has traits or behaviors that are typically associated with children.)

4. **男っぽい / 女っぽい** (manly / womanly)  
   **彼は男っぽい服を着ている。**  
   He wears **manly** clothes.  
   (Refers to clothing that has a "masculine" style.)

   **彼女は女っぽい髪型をしている。**  
   She has a **feminine** hairstyle.

5. **黒っぽい / 白っぽい** (dark-like / light-like)  
   **このシャツは黒っぽい色だ。**  
   This shirt is **dark** (blackish).

   **この壁は白っぽい。**  
   This wall is **light-colored** (whitish).

6. **水っぽい** (watery, too watery)  
   **このスープは水っぽい。**  
   This soup is too **watery** (lacks richness).

---

### **Common Nouns + っぽい**

* **子どもっぽい** – childish, immature
* **男っぽい** – manly
* **女っぽい** – feminine
* **黒っぽい** – dark, blackish
* **白っぽい** – light, whitish
* **水っぽい** – watery

### **Key Points:**

* **っぽい** has a **casual** tone, and it’s typically used in **conversation**.
* It is used to **describe someone's behavior, appearance, or tendencies**, particularly when there is an emphasis on how something "resembles" or "has the characteristics of" another thing.

### **Comparison with other patterns:**

* **～みたいだ**: Similar, but **みたいだ** is often used when making a **guess** based on **appearance** (more subjective or casual). For example: **あの子どもは大人みたいだ** (That child looks like an adult).
* **～らしい**: Describes the **typical** or **expected** traits of something or someone. For example, **大人らしい振る舞い** (adult-like behavior).

---

This grammar is great for casual descriptions, particularly when talking about **people, appearance, and tendencies**. 
It's also often used when talking about **styles or characteristics** that resemble something else.
