# Japanese Daily Communication Sentence Patterns

> Approximately **100–150 grammar patterns** are enough to handle most everyday conversations in Japanese. The list below is organized by category.

---

# 1. Basic Statements

| Meaning | Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|---------|
| This is... | ～です | これはほんです。 |
| This isn't... | ～ではありません | これはほんではありません。 |
| Is this...? | ～ですか | がくせいですか。 |
| It was... | ～でした | きのうはやすみでした。 |
| It wasn't... | ～ではありませんでした | やすみではありませんでした。 |

---

# 2. Existence

| Meaning | Pattern |
|---------|---------|

# Recommended Learning Order

1. Basic sentences (です・ます)
2. Particles (は・が・を・に・で・へ・と・から・まで)
3. Verb tenses
4. て-form and all of its uses
5. Wants & preferences
6. Ability & obligation
7. Reasons & explanations
8. Plans & intentions
9. Conditions & possibilities
10. Common N3 patterns

---

|--------|------------------|----------|
| N5 | 30 | ~50% of daily conversations |
| N4 | 70 | ~80% of daily conversations |
| N3 | 120 | ~95% of daily conversations |
| N2+ | 180+ | Nearly complete everyday fluency |

This is a good reference list. If your goal is **spoken fluency**, I can also reorganize these into a **progressive learning roadmap (about 120 patterns)** where each pattern builds on previous ones in the optimal order, instead of grouping them by topic. That format is much easier to study from and avoids learning advanced patterns before the prerequisites.
