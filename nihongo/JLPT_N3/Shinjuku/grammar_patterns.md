# Japanese Grammar Patterns - JLPT N3

## 1. がち (Gachi)

**Type:** Conjunction

### Meaning
- Often becomes ~; frequently becomes ~
- Often in the state of ~
- The rate/frequency of ~ is high

### Key Points
- Often used for negative or undesirable trends
- Indicates a pattern that occurs repeatedly

### Examples
- **彼は子どもの頃から体が弱く、学校を休みがちだった。**
  - He had been weak since childhood and often missed school.

- **一人暮らしは栄養のバランスがかたよりがちになる。**
  - When you live alone, your nutrition tends to be unbalanced.

- **彼女は仕事が忙しくて、留守がちだ。**
  - She's busy with work and away a lot.

- **最近曇りがちの天気が続いて、洗濯物がなかなか乾かない。**
  - The weather has been cloudy lately, and it's hard to get the laundry dry.

- **これは、三日坊主になりがちな人におすすめの本です。**
  - This is a good book for people who can't stick to anything for very long.

## 2. 気味 (Gimi)

**Type:** Conjunction

### Meaning
- Feels a bit/somewhat ~
- Sense or feeling of ~

### Key Points
- Usually used for negative or undesirable conditions
- Attaches to words describing states or conditions
- **Difference from がち:** "Gachi" is used when a pattern is recognized through repetition; "Gimi" refers only to the current state

### Examples
- **私の時計は最近遅れ気味だ。電池がなくなってきてるのかもしれない。**
  - My watch has been running behind lately. Maybe the batteries are running out.

- **この２、３日、風邪気味でのどが痛い。**
  - I've had a cold and a sore throat for a couple of days.

- **疲れ気味の時ときは、早く寝た方が良い。**
  - If you are feeling tired, you should go to bed earlier.

## 3. げ (~ge)

**Type:** Conjunction

### Meaning
- Appears to be ~; looks like ~
- Describes a person's state of being (somewhat old-fashioned)

### Key Points
- Not often used to describe the condition of superiors (敬語)
- More formal/written expression than ~そう
- Can be replaced with ~そう, but ~げ is more literary

### Examples
- **国の家族の話を聞くと、彼は悲しげな表情になった。**
  - When he heard about his family in the country, he became sad.

- **名前を聞くと、その女の子は恥ずかしげに答えた。**
  - When I asked her name, the girl answered shyly.

- **彼が話している間、彼女は退屈げに窓の外を見ていた。**
  - While he was talking, she was looking out the window in boredom.

## 4. っぽい (~ppoi)

**Type:** Conjunction

### Meaning
- That's how it feels/appears
- Often does ~; tends to ~
- Gets ~ easily

### Key Points
- Expresses the nature of something, not the frequency of actions
- Used with verbs like: 怒る (get angry), 飽きる (get bored), 忘れる (forget)
- Often used for negative characteristics

### Examples
- **弟は飽きっぽくて、何をしても長く続かない。**
  - My brother is easily bored and whatever he does doesn't last long.

- **この鞄は結構高かったのに、安っぽく見える。**
  - This bag was pretty expensive, but it looks cheap.

- **彼は20歳を過ぎているのに、言うことが子どもっぽい。**
  - He's over 20 years old, but what he says is childish.

---

## 5. だらけ (~darakke)

**Type:** Conjunction

### Meaning
- Full of ~; covered in ~
- Filled with (usually negative things like mud, wrinkles, mistakes)

### Examples
- **この書類は間違いだらけで読みにくい。**
  - This document is full of mistakes and hard to read.

- **雨の日にサッカーの試合をしたので、服も靴も泥だらけになってしまった。**
  - We had a soccer game on a rainy day, so our clothes and shoes were covered in mud.

- **彼は、間違いだらけのテストをごみ箱に捨てた。**
  - He threw the test, full of mistakes, into the trash.

---

## 6. っぱなし (~pponashi)

**Type:** Conjunction

### Meaning
- Left in a state of ~; still ~; continuing to do ~

### Key Points
- **Mode 1 (Negligence):** Leaves something in a state without doing what should be done afterward
- **Mode 2 (Continuity):** Continues in a state for an extended period
- Usually carries a negative connotation

### Examples

#### Negligence
- **急いで家を出て、エアコンをつけっぱなしにしてしまった。**
  - I left the house in a hurry and left the air conditioner on.

- **もったいないので、水を出しっぱなしにしないでください。**
  - Do not leave the water running as it is a waste.

- **『散らかしっぱなしの人のための整理術』という本が人気だ。**
  - A book called "Organizing for the Messy" is very popular.

#### Continuity
- **電車が混んでいて、終点までずっと立ちっぱなしだった。**
  - The train was so crowded that I had to stand all the way to the end of the line.

## 7. っけ (~kke)

**Type:** Conjunction

### Meaning
- Used to confirm something unclear or forgotten

### Key Points
- Spoken language primarily
- Usually in the form: past tense + っけ
- Cannot be used with adjectives in past tense form

### Examples
- **明日、山田さんも会議に来る（んだ）っけ。**
  - I remembered that Yamada-san is coming to the meeting tomorrow.

- **先週の日曜日って寒かったっけ。**
  - I don't remember last Sunday being so cold.

- **Aさんってチョコレート好きだったっけ。**
  - Did I mention that Mr. A likes chocolate?

- **今度の漢字テストっていつだっけ？**
  - When's the next kanji test?

## 8. じゃない (~janai)

**Type:** Conjunction

### Meaning
- I think it's probably ~
- Confirmation/emphasis
- Criticism or surprise toward someone

### Key Points
- **Speculation:** Raise intonation at the end
- **Confirmation:** Raise intonation at the end
- **Criticism/Surprise:** Lower intonation at the end
- NOT a negation
- ~じゃん is used similarly but mainly in close relationships

### Examples

**Confirmation/Emphasis:**
- **A: 駅前に新しいレストランできたじゃないですか。あそこ、もう行きました？**
  - A: There's a new restaurant in front of the station. Have you been there yet?
- **B: うん、行きましたよ。結構、おいしかったですよ。**
  - B: Yes, I went there. It was quite tasty.

**Criticism/Surprise:**
- **A: 先生、N3合格しました。**
  - A: Sir, I passed the N3 exam.
- **B: すごいじゃない！おめでとう。**
  - B: That's great! Congratulations.

- **A: 自信ないっていってたけど、歌、上手じゃない。**
  - A: You said you weren't confident, but you're good at singing.
- **B: えー、そんなことないよ。**
  - B: No, I'm not.

---

## Study Tips

1. **Negative Patterns:** がち, 気味, っぽい, だらけ, and っぱなし often describe negative or undesirable conditions
2. **Repetition vs. Current State:** Distinguish between がち (repetition) and 気味 (current state)
3. **Context Matters:** Intonation changes the meaning of じゃない
4. **Natural Speech:** っけ and じゃん are primarily spoken language patterns
